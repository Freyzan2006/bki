Directors

Sadykov A.S. (1973-1987)

The founder of the Institute, Abid Sadykovich Sadykov (1913-1987) - organic chemist, organizer of science and education, public figure, scientist and statesman, Doctor of Chemical Sciences, professor, academician of the Academy of Sciences of the USSR (1972) and Uzbekistan (1947), Honored Worker of Science and Technology of Uzbekistan (1960), Laureate of the State Prize of Uzbekistan named after. Beruni (1979), Hero of Socialist Labor, awarded the Order of Buyuk Khizmatlari Uchun in 2002 (posthumously).

He worked as head of the department of chemistry of natural compounds (1946-1974), director of the Institute of Chemistry of the Academy of Sciences of the UzSSR (1946-1950), rector of Tashkent State University (1958-1966), President of the Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Uzbekistan (1966-1984).

Academician Abid Sadykovich Sadykov was one of the organizers of chemical science in Uzbekistan. His name is associated with the practical application of research into natural compounds of wild flora of Central Asia.

A.S. Sadykov is the initiator of research in a new field of chemical science in Uzbekistan - bioorganic chemistry, new approaches and views based on modern physicochemical and theoretical methods (spectroscopy, quantum chemistry, fine organic synthesis, conformational analysis, mathematical modeling of chemical structures and properties).

Author of more than 500 scientific papers, including 7 monographs.

Trained 10 doctors and more than 80 candidates of science.

Abduvakhabov A.A. (1987-1993)

Doctor of Chemical Sciences (1980), professor (1981), academician (1995) Abduvali Abdusamatovich Abdusamatov is a prominent scientist in the field of organic, bioorganic and organoelement chemistry.

He worked as head of the laboratory of organophosphorus compounds of the Department of Bioorganic Chemistry of the Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Uzbekistan (1977), director of the Institute of Bioorganic Chemistry named after Academician A.S. Sadykov (1987-1993), member of the Presidium of the Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Uzbekistan (1992-200), Academic Secretary (1987-1992), Vice-President of the Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Uzbekistan (1995-1997), awarded the “Inventor of Russia” badge and the silver medal of the Exhibition of Economic Achievements of Russia (1983, 1986), medals “Veteran of Labor” (1988), “Mustakillik” (1992).

Academician A.A. Abduvakhobov created a scientific direction and school of synthesis (more than 7000 compounds), study of the structure, stereochemistry of organophosphorus compounds and the mechanism of interaction of substances with the cholinergic systems of warm-blooded animals and insects.

Under the leadership of Academician A.A. Abduvakhabov, for the first time in the Central Asian region, research was carried out on the isolation, establishment of structure, synthesis, development of a preparative form and use of pest pheromones in field conditions. Currently, cotton and winter bollworm pheromones are widely used in the fields of the Republic to protect cotton from pests.

Scientific activity of Abduvakhabov A.A. included in the biographical issue “Who's Who in Science and Technology”, published in the USA (1996).

Author of more than 300 scientific works, including 5 monographs, 10 copyright certificates and patents.

Trained 7 doctors and 27 candidates of science.

Salikhov Sh.I. (1993-2022)

Shavkat Ismailovich Salikhov is a Soviet and Uzbek scientist in the field of bioorganic chemistry, Doctor of Biological Sciences, professor, academician of the Academy of Sciences of Uzbekistan (1995). Born on December 12, 1944 in Tashkent. Graduated from Tashkent State University (1967). Doctor of Biological Sciences (1984), professor (1989), academician of the Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Uzbekistan (1995). Honored Scientist of the Republic of Uzbekistan (1998), foreign member of the Chinese Academy of Sciences (2017). Awarded the Orders “El-Yurt Khurmati” (2008), “Mehnat Shukhrati” (2014), and the “International Cooperation in the Field of Science and Technology 2017” Award (PRC).

Head of the laboratory (1981), director (1993-2006) of the Institute of Bioorganic Chemistry of the Academy of Sciences of Uzbekistan. From March 2006 to January 2017, President of the Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Uzbekistan. Senator of the Oliy Majlis of the Republic of Uzbekistan (2006-2017). Director of the Institute of Bioorganic Chemistry of the Academy of Sciences of the Republic of Uzbekistan (2017-2022).

He is widely known in the world of science for his work in the field of bioorganic chemistry and biotechnology, and is one of the founders of the school of protein chemistry in the Republic. His scientific works on the study of proteins and peptides from animal venoms are recognized by the wider scientific community. Under the leadership of Salikhov Sh.I. A new technology for the isolation of gossypol has been developed and implemented. A number of drugs have been created - the anti-chlamydial drug Gozalidon, the hemostatic drug Lagoden, the blood substitute Salgivin, the anti-inflammatory drug Apikapsalvin, the immunomodulating drug Timoptin, the drug Ragosin - an antiviral drug for the treatment of viral hepatitis B, C, D and the drug Rutan - an antiviral drug for the treatment of coronavirus infection COVID- 19 of moderate severity and with lung damage up to 30%, as well as against all strains of influenza for adults.

The Academy of Sciences of the People's Republic of China awarded Academician Salikhov Sh.I. "International Scientific Cooperation Award of the Chinese Academy of Sciences" in 2016 for outstanding contributions in the field of science and technology, fruitful cooperation with the Chinese Academy of Sciences, training of talented scientists and for playing an important role in raising the level of scientific research, innovation and development of the Chinese Academy of Sciences through training innovative talents and highly qualified scientists; for his contribution to the organization and implementation of major scientific projects.

Academician Shavkat Ismailovich Salikhov was elected a foreign member of the Chinese Academy of Sciences in 2017 for scientific achievements and contributions to the development of science and technology in China. Sh.I. Salikhov became the first Uzbek citizen and the second scientist from the CIS countries to be elected to the academicianship of the Chinese Academy of Sciences.

Author of more than 450 scientific papers, including 2 monographs, 56 inventions, received more than 50 patents.

Trained 9 doctors and 26 candidates of science.
